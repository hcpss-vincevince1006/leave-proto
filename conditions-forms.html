Qualifying Conditions

What is a qualifying condition?

Eligible employees may take family and medical leave because of:

  • Serious health condition that prevents the employee from performing the essential functions of the employee’s job
  • Birth or placement of a child for adoption or foster care
  • A need to care for the employee’s spouse, child, or parent with a serious health condition
  • Qualifying exigencies arising out of the fact that the employee’s spouse, son, daughter, or parent is on active duty or call to active duty status as a member of the National Guard or Reserves in support of a contingency operation.

What should I do when the baby is born?

Contact Pamela Saulsbury via phone or email with the birth date. Fax a verification of birth and delivery type (regular or cesarean section) to Pamela Saulsbury 410-313-1565(fax). To add the baby to your health insurance, send the verification of birth and Benefits Change Form to the Benefits Office within 30 days after the birth.

If I am expecting a baby, how far in advance of my due date may I stop working?

You may stop working on your own accord within one week of your expected due date. In order to stop working prior to that, you need to submit medical documentation from your physician stating the need to stop working.
